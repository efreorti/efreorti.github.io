Galapagos Weather facts

As the seasons in the Galápagos are split into cool and dry (June to November) and warm and wet (December to June), the best time to visit the Galápagos islands depends on what you want to see. 

Warm season

Is the best time to go to the Galapagos for diving as the sea is calmer and the underwater visibility is really good.

Species Watching

 Animals here can be seen year round, with few migratory species and no need to wander off in search of waterholes or food.

Cool season

Galapagos` weather is not typical of the tropics. The dryer and cooler months lend themselves more to hiking along the coast and through the highlands.

Weather Information

The Galapagos Islands are located within the Eastern Tropical Pacific; there are different characteristics that influence in the climate.

1

Ocean currents & Winds

*The weather is controlled by oceanic currents that surround the islands. The interaction of four warm and cool oceanic currents, create areas of upwelling and strong surface currents.
*Southeast trade winds.
*Climate variability related to the Niño Southern Oscillation phenomena.

2

The Climate

The climate in Galapagos Islands can be misleading, because the lowlands can be very dry during the hot season and the highlands of the islands can be typically wetter during the cold season.

3

The Change

Being that the archipelago is located right along the equator, the Galapagos Islands are often mistakenly thought of as a tropical destination. Yes, the year-round temperatures are quite warm, however, the weather is not typical of the tropics.
